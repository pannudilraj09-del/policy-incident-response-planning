A security policy is a formal document that defines the rules, guidelines, and procedures an organization follows to protect its information systems, networks, and digital assets. It establishes the responsibilities of employees, management, and IT staff in maintaining cybersecurity and ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements. A well-designed security policy helps prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, malware attacks, and insider threats by defining acceptable use, password requirements, access control, data classification, and incident reporting procedures. Security policies also provide a framework for risk management and help organizations maintain business continuity. They are reviewed and updated regularly to address emerging cyber threats and technological changes. By following a strong security policy, organizations can improve security awareness, reduce vulnerabilities, protect sensitive information, and build trust among customers, employees, and business partners.
